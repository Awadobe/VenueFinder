# VenueFind Concept Note

**Document status:** Stage 1 working concept  
**Pilot geography:** Freetown and the wider Western Area, Sierra Leone  
**Primary channel:** Website for discovery; WhatsApp and telephone for confirmation and coordination

## 1. Executive Summary

VenueFind is a venue discovery and availability-confirmation service for people planning weddings, birthdays, workshops, corporate functions, conferences, dinners, and other events in Sierra Leone.

Today, many customers discover venues through personal recommendations, social media, telephone calls, or physical visits. Information about venue capacity, price, facilities, and availability is scattered and can be unreliable. A customer may spend time and transport money visiting a venue only to learn that their preferred date has already been booked.

VenueFind will bring verified venue information into one searchable service. Customers will be able to compare suitable venues, see whether a date is booked, recently confirmed as available, or still requires confirmation, and request help confirming availability or arranging an inspection.

Stage 1 will not operate as an instant-booking marketplace. It will begin as a managed discovery and enquiry service, using a public website supported by manual operations through WhatsApp and telephone. This model is intended to establish trustworthy venue data, learn how venue managers maintain their calendars, and validate willingness to pay before investing in payment processing or complex automation.

## 2. The Problem

People planning events in Freetown and the Western Area face several related difficulties:

- They may know only a small number of venues or depend on recommendations from friends and relatives.
- Venue information is distributed across Facebook, Instagram, WhatsApp, signs, advertisements, and personal contacts.
- Prices, capacity, facilities, booking conditions, and media are rarely presented in a consistent format.
- Customers often call or travel to several locations before finding a suitable venue.
- A preferred venue may already be booked for the required date.
- Advertised prices may not clearly include or exclude chairs, tables, electricity, generator fuel, catering, decoration, parking, security, or cleaning.
- Venue managers may receive bookings through several channels and may not maintain a digital calendar.

The result is lost time, unnecessary transport costs, uncertain planning, and poor comparisons between venues.

## 3. Proposed Service

VenueFind will help customers:

1. Search using their event date, event type, location, expected attendance, and budget.
2. Discover venues they may not otherwise know about.
3. Compare venue capacity, pricing, photographs, videos, amenities, and booking conditions.
4. See a clearly labelled availability status.
5. Request date confirmation or arrange a venue inspection.
6. Receive follow-up through WhatsApp or telephone.

VenueFind's defining value is not merely publishing a directory. Its stronger value is maintaining structured, verified information and helping customers confirm whether a venue is genuinely available.

## 4. Target Customers

### 4.1 Primary customer groups

- Couples and families planning weddings and receptions
- Individuals and families arranging birthdays and private celebrations
- Companies arranging meetings, dinners, launches, and corporate functions
- NGOs, public institutions, schools, and training providers arranging workshops or conferences
- Event planners acting on behalf of clients
- Community and professional groups organizing gatherings

### 4.2 Venue partners

- Dedicated event halls
- Hotel conference and banquet halls
- Restaurants with private event spaces
- Gardens and outdoor venues
- Beaches and resorts offering event spaces
- Community and multipurpose centres
- Guesthouses that provide meeting or event spaces

Ordinary room-only accommodation booking is outside Stage 1. Hotels and guesthouses will be included initially for their event or meeting facilities.

## 5. Value Proposition

### For customers

- Discover more suitable venues in one place.
- Reduce unnecessary travel and repeated telephone enquiries.
- Compare venues using consistent information.
- Avoid pursuing venues already booked for the required date.
- Obtain assistance confirming dates and arranging inspections.

### For venue owners and managers

- Gain visibility among people actively planning events.
- Receive enquiries that already include the date, event type, attendance, and budget.
- Present accurate information and media in a consistent format.
- Reduce repetitive questions from unsuitable customers.
- Gain an additional enquiry and marketing channel without paying for a basic Stage 1 listing.

## 6. Stage 1 Service Scope

### Included

- Public venue discovery and filtering
- Verified venue profiles
- Photographs and short videos
- Capacity, price or price range, location, and event suitability
- Detailed inclusions, exclusions, amenities, and booking conditions
- Availability statuses with verification timestamps
- Date-confirmation requests
- Inspection requests
- WhatsApp and telephone follow-up
- Manual internal management of venue records and customer enquiries
- Free basic venue listings
- Optional featured venue promotion
- Optional customer concierge assistance

### Excluded

- Instant bookings
- Collection of full venue payments
- Automated owner payouts
- Enforced booking commissions
- Hotel-room reservations
- Public ratings and reviews
- Complex self-service owner dashboards
- AI recommendations
- Nationwide coverage at launch

These exclusions protect the pilot from solving technical problems before the underlying service and commercial behaviour have been validated.

## 7. Venue Listing Standard

Every published venue should include, where applicable:

- Venue name and verified location
- Venue category
- Contact person and operating contact details
- Maximum seated and standing capacity
- Suitable event types
- Base price or realistic price range
- Deposit requirement and balance-payment timing
- Cancellation and refund conditions supplied by the venue
- Opening and closing times
- Chairs and tables included or available separately
- Catering policy and kitchen availability
- Decoration rules
- Electricity and generator arrangements, including additional charges
- Parking and road-access information
- Accessibility information
- Toilets and changing-room facilities
- Air conditioning or ventilation
- Security and cleaning arrangements
- Sound, lighting, stage, and projector availability
- Alcohol and noise restrictions
- Photographs and short videos
- Inspection arrangements
- Availability status and last-confirmed timestamp

VenueFind should distinguish information supplied by a venue from information physically or independently verified by the VenueFind team.

## 8. Availability Model

Stage 1 will use three customer-facing states:

- **Available - verified:** The venue confirmed that the date was available within the defined verification period.
- **Booked:** The venue reported that the date was unavailable.
- **Confirmation required:** The record is missing, old, uncertain, or awaiting a response from the venue.

Every status should show when it was last updated. VenueFind must not present old calendar information as guaranteed availability.

### Proposed operating rule

An available date should remain labelled verified only for a short period to be tested during the pilot. If no new confirmation is received within that period, it moves automatically or manually to confirmation required.

A verified-available status is still not a reservation. The customer must receive a separate booking or hold confirmation from the venue.

## 9. Customer Workflow

1. The customer searches by date, event type, location, attendance, and budget.
2. VenueFind returns suitable venue profiles.
3. The customer compares prices, facilities, media, and availability.
4. The customer selects one or more venues and submits an enquiry.
5. VenueFind checks that the enquiry contains enough information.
6. VenueFind contacts the venue manager when confirmation is required.
7. VenueFind sends the customer the response and, where appropriate, arranges an inspection.
8. The venue and customer follow the venue's deposit and contracting process.
9. VenueFind follows up to record whether the enquiry resulted in an inspection or booking.

Customers should not initially be sent away with only a telephone number. Remaining involved through confirmation and inspection gives VenueFind a measurable service role and creates better information about enquiry outcomes.

## 10. Venue Partner Workflow

1. VenueFind identifies a venue or receives a listing request.
2. The owner or authorized manager supplies the required information and media.
3. VenueFind verifies the venue, contact person, and core listing details.
4. VenueFind publishes an approved basic listing.
5. The venue sends availability updates through WhatsApp or another agreed process.
6. VenueFind sends structured, qualified enquiries to the designated contact.
7. The venue responds within an agreed service period.
8. VenueFind records confirmed availability, inspections, and reported booking outcomes.

An unverified venue may be stored internally but should not be presented to customers as a verified listing.

## 11. Proposed Revenue Model

### Stage 1 defaults

- Customers search and view venues for free.
- Venues receive a basic listing for free.
- Venues may pay for clearly labelled featured placement or promotional packages.
- Customers may pay for an optional concierge service involving research, shortlisting, multiple availability checks, inspection coordination, or package comparison.
- VenueFind will not initially depend on booking commission revenue.

### Why this model is recommended

Charging customers simply to view search results would create friction while free discovery alternatives still exist. Charging every venue before VenueFind has demonstrated demand would also be difficult to justify. Featured promotion and concierge work provide identifiable value while the marketplace develops.

A booking commission may be tested later, but it is vulnerable to customers and venues completing transactions outside the platform. It should not be treated as guaranteed revenue until VenueFind controls or reliably tracks more of the booking process.

### Commercial assumptions requiring validation

- Venue owners will value qualified enquiries and measurable visibility.
- Some owners will pay for featured placement after VenueFind demonstrates customer traffic.
- A segment of time-sensitive or busy customers will pay for concierge assistance.
- Venue managers will report booking outcomes consistently enough to measure conversions.

## 12. Pilot Plan

### Proposed pilot parameters

- **Geography:** Freetown and wider Western Area
- **Duration:** Eight weeks of live customer testing after venue onboarding
- **Venue target:** 20-30 verified venues
- **Venue mix:** Affordable, mid-range, and premium
- **Channel:** Public mobile-friendly website supported by WhatsApp and telephone
- **Operations:** Manual venue verification, calendar updates, and enquiry coordination

### Venue recruitment approach

The pilot should recruit a balanced selection rather than only highly visible premium venues. Recruitment should cover different capacities, prices, event types, and areas. The initial proposition to owners is a free verified listing and structured customer enquiries in exchange for accurate information and timely availability responses.

## 13. Pilot Success Measures

Stage 1 should measure service behaviour, not only website traffic.

### Supply measures

- Number of verified venues published
- Percentage of listings with complete required information
- Percentage with sufficient current photographs
- Frequency of availability updates
- Median venue response time to confirmation requests
- Percentage of venue partners responding within the agreed period

### Customer measures

- Number of genuine venue searches and enquiries
- Percentage of searches producing at least three suitable venues
- Enquiry-to-confirmation rate
- Confirmation-to-inspection rate
- Inspection-to-reported-booking rate
- Customer-reported time or trips saved
- Common unsuccessful-search reasons, including budget, capacity, location, and date

### Commercial measures

- Number of owners willing to test paid promotion
- Revenue from featured placement
- Number of concierge requests
- Revenue and delivery cost per concierge request
- Estimated cost of acquiring and servicing each enquiry

Page views and social-media reach may be monitored, but they are not sufficient evidence that the service solves the problem.

## 14. Key Risks and Controls

### Inaccurate availability

**Risk:** Customers lose trust when an apparently available venue is already booked.  
**Control:** Use timestamps, three-state availability, short verification windows, and manual reconfirmation.

### Venue managers fail to update calendars

**Risk:** Records become outdated because bookings arrive through other channels.  
**Control:** Use simple WhatsApp updates, scheduled check-ins, and response-performance tracking.

### Customers and venues bypass VenueFind

**Risk:** VenueFind cannot track outcomes or collect commissions.  
**Control:** Do not rely on commissions during Stage 1; create value through confirmation, coordination, concierge work, and promotion.

### Incomplete or misleading venue information

**Risk:** Advertised prices omit important costs or media misrepresents the venue.  
**Control:** Apply a listing standard, record inclusions and exclusions, and label the verification source.

### Expansion weakens the core service

**Risk:** Adding accommodation, national coverage, payments, and other categories prevents reliable execution.  
**Control:** Maintain the Stage 1 boundary until the Freetown venue-discovery workflow is proven.

## 15. Decisions Locked for Stage 1

The working model adopts the following defaults:

1. VenueFind launches in Freetown and the wider Western Area.
2. The core market is event venues, not general accommodation booking.
3. Venue search is free for customers.
4. Basic listings are free for venue owners during the pilot.
5. Listings and venue contacts are verified before publication.
6. Availability is presented as verified available, booked, or confirmation required.
7. VenueFind remains involved through date confirmation and inspection coordination.
8. Website discovery is supported by WhatsApp and telephone operations.
9. Stage 1 revenue tests focus on featured promotion and optional concierge service.
10. Instant booking, payment collection, commissions, and complex automation are deferred.

## 16. Decisions to Validate During the Pilot

These questions should be answered with pilot evidence rather than assumptions:

1. How frequently must an available date be reconfirmed to remain trustworthy?
2. How quickly will venue managers respond to VenueFind enquiries?
3. Which event types and price ranges generate the strongest demand?
4. Which listing information most affects customer decisions?
5. Will customers pay for concierge assistance, and for which specific tasks?
6. Will owners pay for featured placement after seeing enquiry results?
7. What constitutes a qualified enquiry for venue partners?
8. Should VenueFind later support temporary date holds or request-to-book transactions?
9. What verification badge or wording can be supported operationally without overstating certainty?

## 17. Immediate Next Steps

1. Interview prospective customers who have recently searched for an event venue.
2. Interview venue owners and managers about enquiry handling and calendar practices.
3. Create the venue onboarding form and verification checklist.
4. Create the customer enquiry and inspection-request form.
5. Set up the internal venue, availability, and enquiry records.
6. Recruit and verify the first pilot venues.
7. Revise the existing prototype to match the Stage 1 service boundary.
8. Begin the eight-week live pilot only after a useful initial venue selection is ready.

## 18. Positioning Statement

> VenueFind helps people discover and compare verified event venues in Freetown, check date availability, and arrange enquiries or inspections without travelling from venue to venue.
